# Phishing Case Study
**Task 5 Part A: Cybercrime Awareness (CSE0001 Digital Literacy)**  


## What is Phishing?
Phishing is a cybercrime where attackers impersonate trusted organizations to steal sensitive information like passwords, UPI PINs, or bank details through fake emails/SMS with malicious links.

## How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step):
1. **Fake Alert**: SMS like "PhonePe: Your UPI payment failed. Verify now" or "Scholarship approved - claim here"
2. **Malicious Link**: Shortened URL (bit.ly/adf123) or QR code 
3. **Fake Login Page**: Replica of PhonePe/VIT portal asking for credentials
4. **Data Stolen**: Victim enters UPI PIN/bank details
5. **Funds Drained**: ₹10,000-50,000 transferred instantly

## Who Gets Targeted?
College students aged 18-24 using UPI apps daily. NCRB 2024 data shows students as 35% of 1.2 lakh+ phishing victims.

## **REAL CASE (Source: The Hindu, Feb 2025)**:
**"20 engineering students in Tamil Nadu lost ₹3.2 lakhs** to phishing SMS claiming 'IRCTC ticket booking confirmation' during semester exams. Attackers sent links to fake IRCTC login pages. Students entered UPI credentials, leading to immediate GPay/PhonePe transfers. One student (VIT Vellore 2nd year) lost ₹45,000 scholarship money. Police recovered only 20% after 3 months." [Source: The Hindu, 12 Feb 2025]

**Consequences**: Financial loss, frozen accounts, academic stress, damaged credit scores.

**Key Lesson**: Real organizations never ask for UPI PIN via SMS/email.
